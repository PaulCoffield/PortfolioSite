Who is Paul Coffield?

For as long as I can remember I’ve been a creative thinker. For even longer I’ve been curious about everything (nosey bugger). When I was first introduced to User Experience Design as a field, I saw it as a collision of these two facets of myself and I’ve been working away to make the most of that ever since.

I’ve done some time in the trenches as a website designer & frontend developer so I’m comfortable enough with writing and investigating HTML/CSS markup, debugging JS - Hell! I’m sure I’ve even written some SQL in my time too!

My time spent as a developer, along with my design education has meant that I find myself able to work well as a 'UX Team of One’ within a number of development teams.

Understanding People

I'm a people person (chatty guy), I genuinely enjoy spending time listening to people and have on one, or more, occasions turned that into a mild interrogation. In a nice way. I’m passionate about what I do, so when someone else is passionate in their response to my question/s - it gets me fired up to ask more and more.

Working as a UX Designer allows me to capitalise on this - I get to speak with people and at least try to understand what they are doing, why they are doing it, where it happens and so on. I then get to take this information and factor it into the visual & interaction design work I carry out, making sure that I do what I can to make things better for people, making it easier for them to achieve their goals.

Personal Development

I have a high level of Personal Pride. It is a core belief of mine to always be improving - learning new skills/software, understanding broader subjects, drilling down into detail. Whatever for it takes, personal development is something I believe we should all strive after for ourselves.

It’s not too difficult to admit when you don’t know something - make learning new things a personal goal and every failure turns into an opportunity to improve.
